
**User Story# = US# **
## Executive summary 

- Sprint 1 delivered all 5 committed user stories for the Conference Room Booking System, achieving the sprint goal of a functional core booking experience. Key process wins included pair programming and mid-sprint PO demos, while external dependency management and reviewer assignment emerged as areas for improvement in Sprint 2.

## Key accomplishments 
- Room discovery fully functional: Users can view all rooms, filter by capacity, and check availability by date (US#1, US#2, US#3). This represents the most-used workflow identified in stakeholder interviews.

- Authentication implemented: Secure login and session management (US#4) is complete, enabling all future stories to build on a stable identity layer.

- Confirmation page delivered: The booking confirmation UI (US#7) was descoped to front-end only by agreement and completed, providing a visual foundation for Sprint 2 back-end integration.


## Challenges faced 
- Late-breaking validation bug in US#5: Time-slot conflict logic was not covered by agreed acceptance criteria until mid-sprint. The bug was introduced on Day 7, discovered on Day 9, and could not be resolved before the review

- Integration testing deferred too late: Front-end and back-end components were not integrated and tested together until the final two days, reducing the time available to catch and resolve issues.

- Undocumented scope change: The verbal agreement to reduce US#7 scope was not recorded, causing confusion during the review and a brief loss of stakeholder trust.

## Process improvements identified 
- Confirm all acceptance criteria — especially edge cases — before a story is pulled into a sprint.

- Enforce a WIP limit of 2 stories in progress simultaneously.

- Introduce a mandatory integration test checkpoint at the sprint midpoint (Day 5).

- Record any scope change on the board within 1 hour of the decision.

- Use timeboxed spikes before estimating stories with external or unknown technical dependencies.
