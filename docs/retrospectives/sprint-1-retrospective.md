# Sprint 1 Retrospective 
Date: 16 May 2026 

Participants: Syke (Product Owner), Xolisa (Scrum Master), Kamuhelo (Backend Lead), Tebza (Frontend), Lisa(Full-stack), Zethu (QA)


## Start - Practices or behaviours to introduce next sprint 

- Writing acceptance criteria before development begins.The   team should confirm all acceptance criteria in a brieflying session before a story enters the sprint. 

- Daily stand-up blockers board. Blockers were discussed verbally but not tracked visibly. A simple shared column in the task board (physical or digital) for active blockers would make impediments visible to the whole team sooner.

- Timeboxed spike stories for technical unknowns. The email confirmation feature (User Story#6) was estimated without understanding the third-party email service integration. A short spike (max half a day) should be run before estimating any story with external dependencies.

## Stop - Practices or behaviours that hindered progress 

-Picking up new stories without completing in-progress ones.

- Leaving integration testing to last minutes.Integration testing should happen continuously, not as a final gate

- All scope changes must be recorded on the board immediately.

## Continue - Practices that worked well and should be maintained 

- Practices that worked well and should be maintained

- Continue early PO demos mid-sprint (Story #1 demo on Day 5 validated direction and prevented rework)

- Team-authored Definition of Done. The shared DoD (code reviewed, unit tested, acceptance criteria verified, demo-ready) gave the team a clear quality bar and prevented partially done work from being called complete. Continue enforcing it strictly.
